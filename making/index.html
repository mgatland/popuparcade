The Pop-Up Guide to Making Games

There are lots of great tools to help you get started making games.

Here are some of our favourites:

  • Scratch – make simple stories, games and animations.
  • Twine – make interactive stories. This is a great place to start if you like writing.
  • Gamefroot – make simple action games and other 2D games.
  • Gamemaker – a more complicated tool for making 2D games.

You'll need a computer to use these tools. If you don't have a computer, you might want to get some paper and pens and design board games instead.

The best way to improve your games is to let people play them. See what they enjoy, and where they get stuck or become frustrated. This will help you make your game more accessible and fun.
